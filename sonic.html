Starting as nothing more than a way to compete with Nintenod's own Mario, Sonic's popularity soon skyrocketed, from 1991 to 1994, as his most famous games, Sonic the Hendgehog, Sonic the Hedgehog 2, Sonic the Hedgehog 3 and so on, gave players fast paced platforming that was revolutionary at the time.

In 1998 Sonic made the big jump from 2D platforming to 3D, bringing with him the high speed gameplay the series was known for in the Sonic Adventure games.

In recent years Sonic has appeared in racing games, and event went back to the 2D style of gameplay the series was known for in Sonic Rush, Sonic Colors and even in the new unreleased Sonic Mania game. Sonic's newer games have been #d platformers including the Sonic Boom series.
